The thesis

Most router papers argue quality with a leaderboard number. That hides where a method helps or hurts, and you can’t check it from the paper alone — a pattern we call masking. Compitum takes the opposite stance, verifiable by construction: it routes by minimizing a scalarized Symbolic Free Energy over a learned SPD (Mahalanobis) metric, under feasibility-first constraints, with a Lyapunov-style controller gating two-timescale online updates — and every structural and dynamical claim is pinned to a property-based test you can run to try to break it.

Falsification harness (P1–P8)

The honest empirical reading

E1 — a real, narrow win

On a synthetic benchmark, Compitum attains higher utility-per-dollar than fixed/greedy/bandit baselines at zero constraint violations — at a real latency cost (~6 s p95 vs sub-millisecond). The geometry pays off when correctness and cost-efficiency matter more than raw decision latency.

E2 — a negative result (and a prediction)

On flat, engineered-feature materials data the curved-metric advantage disappears (scaling exponent α ≈ 1, metric ≈ Euclidean). A metric is only meaningful on the right chart; the method is not expected to manufacture curvature where the data are flat. Stating this is the point, not the embarrassment.

Scope & program

Claimed: a concrete construction; local, surrogate, test-matched Lyapunov-style non-increase; a falsification harness; an honest empirical reading. Not claimed: a general benchmark win, a global stability proof, or that the free energy is the “correct” potential. Those are open — the direction the program is heading.

This artifact is one bloom of a program organized by a single commitment — adequacy should be measurable, not asserted. Sibling work: The Wicked Prior (bounded-observer geometry), and the broader program at compitum.space.
